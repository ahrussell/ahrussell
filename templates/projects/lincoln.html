    LincolnBot was originally small project used primarily to get myself more involved with some basic AI programming (using Markov chains) and exploring some structure in language. I was also taking a class in American Political Thought at the time, taught by the amazing Professor Susan McWilliams, and thought I could use this as an extra credit assignment if accompanied by some analysis.

    Naive Markov chains worked reasonably well, and after I found myself trying to build a Part-of-Speech tagging system to refine my result, Professor Sara Sood was kind enough to point me to Python's Natural Language Toolkit. While not perfect, I am reasonably happy with how the project turned out. I am definitely interested in pursuing language parsing and analysis more (for example, comparing the speeches and writing of Lincoln and Obama).

    The program takes a number of Lincoln's works and forms nth-order Markov chains (specified before runtime) and then uses these to produce a number of sentences. Higher values of n will give more direct quotes from the source material. I'd recommend one of about 3. Check it out for yourself!
